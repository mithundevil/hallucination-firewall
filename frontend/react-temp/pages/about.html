Project Architecture & Design

A deep dive into how the Hallucination Firewall protects multimodal systems.

Problem Statement

Generative AI models often produce "hallucinations" â€” outputs that are plausible but incorrect. In multimodal contexts, this includes synthetically generated images that pass for real and text that makes over-confident, false claims. Existing firewalls focus on network security, leaving a gap in "Semantic Security".

Our Objectives

  • Detect AI-generated images using structural noise analysis.
  • Identify linguistic markers of hallucination in text.
  • Provide an explainable risk score (XAI) for every scan.
  • Maintain a persistent ledger of all security audits.

System Flow

01
Input Stage

User provides image/text payload for verification.

02
Analysis Stage

FFT, Laplacian, and Rule-based text scoring.

03
Decision Stage

Firewall aggregates risks into a final verdict.

04
Audit Stage

Result is logged in the persistent history manager.

Core Algorithms

Image: We use Fast Fourier Transform (FFT) to detect unnatural frequency spikes and Laplacian Variance to calculate image "smoothness" characteristic of AI GANs.

Text: Heuristic analysis of over-confidence words (absolute markers) and lack of hedging in long-form responses.
